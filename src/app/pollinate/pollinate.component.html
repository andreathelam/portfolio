OVERVIEW

Pollinate is a mobile gardening tool that provides easy to understand and personalized care guides, connects new and experienced gardeners, and presents selected recipes with ingredients in their gardens to give incentive for users to take care of their plants and themselves.

This project was part of CreateSC, 24 hour designathon, where the given prompt was to design a mobile app that focuses on user wellness. Out of 60+ projects entered, our team placed 1st!

Defining the Problem

How does gardening relate to wellness?

To better understand how effective gardening is to one's wellness, we conducted research online and found:

  • Viewing plants altered EEG recordings and reduced stress, fear, anger and sadness, as well as reducing blood pressure, pulse rate and muscle tension. — Clinical Medicine (London, England)

  • Gardening can build self-esteem by accomplishing new tasks. — UNC Health Talk

  • Gardening helps you eat more fresh fruits and vegetables, and you get to decide what kinds of fertilizers and pesticides come in contact with your food. — Harvard Health Publishing

Method: Interviews

We wanted to understand what our peers think about gardening and how approachable the activity is, so we conducted interviews with 4 young adults.

Research Questions

    1. What is your experience with gardening?
    2. Why do you garden or want to garden?
    3. What tasks do you do when gardening?
    4. What are some frustrations you have with gardening?
    5. Are you in a gardening community/know other people who garden? Is it hard to find others?
    6. What would make gardening easier for you?

The Problem

Even though gardening is a productive hobby, contributing to both physical and mental health, it can be hard for young adults to start and stick with it.

Persona

With what we learned from the research, we created three personas in three audience groups: Gardening Newbie, Community Gardener, and Experienced Gardener.

From the user research, we decided that the app would incorporate three core concepts that tackle the frustrations of the user groups and serve their gardening goals: Resources, Recipes, and Community

Putting it All Together

Final Mockups

Onboarding

When the user first enters, they will be guided through a few questions so that the app will be catered towards their gardening expertise and needs.

Resources for Plant Care

The user can browse and add plants to their virtual garden. Each plant will have it own page with care instruction and tips. Alongside, there is a schedule assistant to track plant wellness and remind when a plant needs watering. If any plants are not doing well, a camera feature will allow the user to take a picture in which an algorithm will use to diagnose the problem.

Recipes

Recipes will be generated based off of the plants in the user's garden.

Community Corner

In the community corner, the user can connect and interact with other gardeners that share the same interests.

Takeaways & Next Steps

Given the short amount of time, we weren't able to dive deep into the user research and practice iterative design. With more time, I would have expanded the research to more age groups to validate that our product can connect to a wider audience. Furthermore, I would have conducted user testing and walkthroughs with the prototype so that the design can be improved and refined. Nevertheless, I am proud of what my team was able to accomplish within 24 hours and that I got to work on a project that I am passionate and advocate for.
