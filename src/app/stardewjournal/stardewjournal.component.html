OVERVIEW

Stardew Valley is an open-world RPG where players play as a corporate-employee-turned-farmer that has inherited their grandfather’s land in Pelican Town. The entertaining storyline and quests and adorable pixel-art sucked me in, and soon, this game became my obsession but also a source of stress.

As a virgo-rising, I had the urge to plan every activity and detail of my days within the game. However, each day was only 15 minutes long in real-time, so this resulted in many unorganized scribbles on post-it notes and me frantically googling the hours of Robin’s shop before time was up. So, I wanted to see if other players faced the same problems as me and to figure out a solution.

Stardew Journal is a management app tool specifically to help players plan and manage their gameplay. The goal is to gather all Stardew Valley materials and information in one place for users to easily personalize and create lists that cater towards their needs.

Understanding the User

Survey

To understand the perspectives and needs from other players, I conducted a survey and posted it on the Stardew Valley subreddit. The responses I received covered a wide range of players from the console, mobile, and pc platforms and from people who have been playing for 2 months to 4 years.

What I learned from the Survey


Personas

Asking for the processes players used when planning their tasks and managing their farms, I categorized the responses into 3 general groups based on what aspect of the game is focused on and the players’ needs. These are the crafted personas.


What I learned from the Personas

With so many aspects of the game players can focus on, there were a variety of wants and needs. However, the most common need was the use of lists to plan and organize their gameplay. Therefore, I focused on incorporating the ability to create multiple types of lists and the flexibility to group and customize those lists. Moreover, nearly all survey participants referred to the Stardew Wiki page for gameplay information such as bundle items and NPC likes/dislikes; therefore, I planned to include that information directly on the application.

Laying the Foundation

Requirements

  • Widgets Interface
    • The user can choose from a set list of templates (community bundles, characters, etc) to add to their home view or to add their own lists as a widget.
  • Calendar View
    • The Calendar view will include birthdays and festival dates from each season. The user will also be able to add notes onto the calendar.
  • Create lists of multiple types
    • The user will have the option to create different kinds of lists, chooseing from checklists, bullets, and numbered. These lists will include a title and the folder it’s in.
  • Folder and subfolders
    • The user will be able to place their lists in folders and sub-folders for organization.
  • Reminders
    • The user will be able to add to the reminders lists that will be easily accessible from the tab menu

Putting it All Together

Hi-Fi Wireframes

Widget Home Screen

The user will be able to customize and personalize their Home screens to display easy-to-view and easy-to-access widgets for important information and lists. When making any edits, there will be a pop-up modal that will confirm their decision.

Calendar View

Stardew Journal will provide information, such as NPCs’ birthdays and festival days on each season’s calendar. The user will also have the ability to add their own to-dos or events onto each date.

Create Lists (Bullet, Numbered, Checkbox)

The user will be able to create their own lists and organize them in folders. For decorative and familiarity purposes, they will also have the ability to insert icons and images of the various items from the game.

Pre-made Templates

The user will have an option to add pre-made templates with the information of NPCs and Community Center bundles. These lists can be added into a folder or on the home screen as a widget. By providing this information, the user will not need to do their own research and can use these lists to record their progress.

Reminders

Stardew Journal will automatically generate a list of reminders of any unchecked items from the user's notes. Clicking on each reminder will lead them to the list.

Takeaways

As I was transitioning from the low-fi wireframes to hi-fi wireframes, I referred back to the task flows often. This taught me the importance of planning the interactions and decisions the user can take, in order to determine the key pages for the application. Since this application was designed to be a flexible management tool, there were many different approaches to how list data could be displayed, and the design I created was aimed to have a balance of flexibility and some structure. My next steps would be to conduct user testing to determine whether the flow of the application is understandable and familiar.
