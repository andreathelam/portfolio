OVERVIEW

Warhawk (2007) is a multiplayer third-person shooter video game on the Playstation 3. It was one of the first shooting games I remember playing as a kid with my older brother. A few years ago, Sony took the servers offline, but the love and support around the game was so strong that the servers were revived. Going through the screens of Warhawk brought back memories and the realization of how much has changed with the user interfaces of shooting games. In this project, I wanted to explore how the game would look with a modern-day approach.

Original

The style of boxes and borders is outdated. The table does not display enough information out front for the player to make a decision without having to toggle through each servers’ details.

Redesign

The redesign includes an additional screen for the player to select the game mode they want to browse. That will lead them to the server list which includes the additional information of the map layout, current time, and current score. The rest of the server information could be displayed as a pop-up modal when the player clicks triangle.

Player Customization

Original

The player customization settings are difficult to navigate since the player would have to toggle through and recall hundreds of different styles.

Redesign

The redesign provides a visual aid for the player to recognize the various styles. It also organizes the features into categories for a cleaner layout.

HUD

Original

The HUD has no visual hierarchy and the components on the screen are not clear or easy to understand.

Redesign

The redesign places the player’s health and stats at the bottom left and the game stats at the top left. It includes a visual bar for the ammo remaining for each weapon.
