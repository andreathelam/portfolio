Leaderboards

Leaderboards is new feature that encourages players to compete in various categories and modes in order to claim exclusive titles and/or rewards. I was handed the UX design document and tasked with converting the low/medium fidelity wireframes into pixel-perfect, game-ready mockups while following the New World visual style.


Landing Page

This is the landing page where players can see all the categories and subcategories. Each panel will lead the player to its respective leaderboard spreadsheet. I went through multiple passes at the layout of the panels to determine which composition would be the most readable and visually-inviting. As a note, game designers wanted emphasis on Faction Wars and Mutated Expeditions.

Utimately, we decided on a three column (1:2:1) approach as it presented the two main categories clearly and the ratio provided balance across the screen. I included bold, large imagery and icons of the subcategories with a common frame backing to tie it all together.

Ping System UX Review

I was tasked with a UX analysis of the game's current ping system to understand any frustrations players are facing and how it can be improved to provide smoother gameplay.

The ping system allows players to quickly communicate with other players using markers in the game world. This can be used to alert teammates to important information, such as enemy position, assistance requests, and location of loot.

Current System

The game has 10 types of pings which reside in a wheel that players can pull up. Once making a selection, players can aim at an area in the world and the ping marker will appear, visible to others.

Usability Issues I gathered:

  • Size - small and short, difficult to see in the world
  • Color - only two colors are used
  • Sounds - lack of distinct sounds to differentiate urgency
  • Recognition - difficulty to distingish on wheel quickly and the icon on the marker in the world is hidden unless the player hovers over it
  • Iconography - symbols for Move, Need Help, and Look are ambiguous
  • Visual Art - art style of wheel does not match the game's branding

Exploration in Other Games

League of Legends, Overwatch, Apex Legends

  • Uses distinct colors for each ping type
  • Ping types are labeled directly on wheel
  • Markers in the world are high up and at a large, visible size
  • Pings prompt a voice line to the team and shows a notification at the top right


Design Solutions

I dove deep into improvements for recognizability. Though the diamond frame provides consistency, without it, the symbols can be displayed at larger size on the ping wheel.

Color-coding each ping type will help with distinction, but I wanted to make sure the colors chosen would stand out against various environments in the game and also be recognizable to those with a colorblind accessiblity.

Changes Implemented

  • Pings in the world will always display the ping symbol at a taller and larger size
  • New icons for Here, Look, Help
  • Remove the diamond frame behind the ping icon on the wheel but remain in world to prevent the ping from blending into the world environments
  • Each ping type will have its own distinct color

Other Changes Proposed but not Implemented

Due to time constraints and lack of bandwidth from engineering, my full list of proposals did not fit the scope. I prioritized UX changes based on how impactful and desirable it would be for players in their gameplay, taking into account how long each task would take to implement. I hope to revisit these improvements for this system in the near future.

  • Reduce the number of pings
    • Collected telemetry data discovered that players used Loot, Repair, and Someone's Here the least
  • Add unique sounds for each ping type or voice the ping type
  • Include labels directly on the wheel next to each icon
  • Change visual treatment to fit into the game's style
